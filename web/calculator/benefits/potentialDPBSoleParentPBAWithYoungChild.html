Based on your answers you may be able to get Domestic Purposes Benefit, a weekly payment of up to $$dpbRate which helps sole parents with one or more dependent children.

Before you can get the Domestic Purposes Benefit you will need to demonstrate that you are actively looking for work.

Details about when a payment can start are available here.

When you call us to arrange an appointment we will ask you to complete one or more of the activities below.


Search for a job
Most jobs are never advertised - they go straight to the people who actively look for them.
Remember to keep records of:
  • Acknowledgement of job applications (online or post)
  • Confirmation of any job interviews arranged
  • Correspondence of any application outcomes

Attend a Work Search Seminar
Held in Work and Income service centres, these seminars give you information about local job opportunities and tips about how you can make your job search more successful.

Complete your employment & skills profile
Work and Income can promote you to employers. It also allows employers to browse your profile and contact you directly if they are interested in you.

Produce a CV
Your CV has one role - to get you an interview. So you only need a short summary to say who you are and what skills, experience, training and qualifications you have.

Attend an appointment with a case manager to apply for benefit, complete a plan, and to discuss employment

Full details about these activities and other useful job search tips can be found here

You are expected to be actively looking and available for work, both before and while in receipt of the Widow's Benefit.
